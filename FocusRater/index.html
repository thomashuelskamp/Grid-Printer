How sharpness is measured

The dense black bar of tick lines is located by finding the image region with the highest Laplacian variance — the sharpest spatial frequency content. The bar is then scanned slice by slice along the scale axis. Each slice's Laplacian variance [0,1,0 / 1,−4,1 / 0,1,0] is the local sharpness score, mapped to a scale value using the two label positions. The result is a sharpness profile showing which scale values correspond to high, medium, and low optical resolution — revealing the exact focus range of your imaging setup.

HIGH — crisp edges, strong Laplacian response MED — visible but soft edges LOW — blurred, weak edge response
